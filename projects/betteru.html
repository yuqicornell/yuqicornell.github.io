BetterU Mobile App

A health mobile application that uses persuasive design to
promote health eating behavior

  1. Home
  2. Portfolio
  3. BetterU

Time: August – December 2014

Team Size: 5

Role: UX Researcher

Methods: Persuasive Design, Surveys, Qualitative Coding, Quantitative Analysis, Secondary research

Product: BetterU mobile android application, Academic paper in the ACM Special Interest Group on Human-Computer Interaction (SIGCHI) conference format

Abstract

An unhealthy diet and food habits can contribute to obesity and can put individuals at risk for chronic illnesses. We developed and evaluated a mobile Android application that aims to prime users to eat healthier by systematically switching their Android wallpaper and lock screen image to a photo of a healthy meal for a limited period of time. The initial study could not conclude whether application improved healthy eating, but positive user experience results and user data captured during the study recommend future research using this approach. Our work contributes to existing knowledge as it examines the potential for a passive, mobile, preference-based image priming approach as a method for influencing healthy eating behavior.
