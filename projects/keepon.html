MyKeepon Project

Help people improve their emotional regulation skills by interacting with Keepon robot

  1. Home
  2. Portfolio

Time: May – December 2015

Team Size: 5

Role: UX Researcher Arduino Developer

Methodology: Literature review, User Testing, User Interviews, Behavior Mapping, Design Thinking, Prototyping, Wizard of Oz, Arduino Programming, 3-D Printing

Tools: Pen & paper, Arduino

Product: A fully functional Keepon robot and a complete exploratory human-robot interaction study.

Overview

This project is to design human-robot interactions using socially assistive robot - Keepon. The purpose of this exploratory study is to see how robot’s empathy behaviors can help college students regulate their emotions.

Keepon Robot

For this study, we used an open-source robot - Keepon. One reason we select this robot is because it has great degree of freedoms in movement. With Keepon, we can easily simulate human behaviors, such as nodding, shaking head, idly looking, and breath. Keepon is also capable in conveying human emotions, such as happy, sad, and excitement.

In order to build a deep empathy and understanding of our users, our team went to eight local restaurants to observe ongoing kitchen activities. This selection of kitchens covers different cuisines, open or closed kitchen types, fast food or sit-down restaurant types, and on-campus or off-campus. With permission, I conducted contextual inquiries for two restaurants. Those highly detailed observations allow us to define our focus without pre-assumptions and design what they really need.

Also by observing different kinds of restaurants, we discovered that ingredient containers (figure below) are widely used across a variety of restaurants like fast food and Chinese. I will discuss the significance of this observation in the following sections.

Keepon’s interaction with kids in Sciencenter, Ithaca NY

We presented our robot to children around 10 years old in Sciencenter, Ithaca. It is an educational activity of teaching children robotics, but the enthusiasm children showed towards Keepon robot gave us great confidence moving forward.

Lab User Study

We also designed user studies in laboratory setting. Experimenters controlled Keepon from another room using Wizard of Oz method. By observing user’s behavior, experimenter issues Keepon corresponding movements commands. We used different psychological and behavioral measurements to evaluate the emotional responses of our participants.
